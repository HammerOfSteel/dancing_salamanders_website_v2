*Daffodil* is a novel set in Crosskeys and Newport, South Wales.

---

## Excerpt

The cottage in Crosskeys had low ceilings and thick walls and the kind of quiet that is not emptiness but fullness — the quiet of something that has held many lives and is ready to hold another.

Steph stood at the kitchen window watching the garden. February, still. The daffodils were coming but hadn't come yet — just the green shoots insisting upward through the cold ground. She had been counting them for three weeks.

"They'll be out by the time she gets here," Eira said from the doorway.

"How do you know it's a she?"

Eira smiled. "I don't. But I have a feeling."

---

The drive to the Royal Gwent was ordinary in the way that only the most extraordinary moments are — the A467, the roundabout, the long straight towards Newport in the very early morning when the town was still quiet and the streetlights were still on. Steph looked out of the window and thought: *this is the last morning that will be like this*.

She was right.

Tima arrived at 6:47 in the morning on the eighteenth of March, as the daffodils in the cottage garden came fully into bloom.

---

*Daffodil* tells the story of expectation and arrival, of what it means to wait for someone you already love. It is the second in a series set in the South Wales valleys.

*Author: Eric Iron*
