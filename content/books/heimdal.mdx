*Heimdal* is a novel in scenes — a travelogue of small moments between a father and daughter, tracing a journey from South Wales to Östersund in Sweden.

---

## Excerpt

The bus from Risca to Newport always smelled of damp coats and the particular tired quality of a Friday afternoon. Tima sat on my lap and pointed out everything through the window with the authority of a person conducting an inventory of the world.

"Tree!"

"Big bin!"

"House! Blue car! Two dog!"

"Two dogs? You sure?"

She nodded fiercely. "One sit. One bark."

I laughed and kissed the top of her head. "You've got good eyes."

---

The canal path in Cwmcarn was ours. It always had been — the way that particular places belong to particular people, not through ownership but through the quality of attention you bring to them. Tima walked ahead with Hoppy tucked under one arm and a stick in the other hand, narrating her progress in the half-commentary, half-song that was her default mode of moving through the world.

The daycare fence was ahead. I could hear the other children before I saw them.

Tima stopped and looked at me.

"Eira?"

"Not today, love. Today it's just us."

She considered this. "Okay," she said. "I'm the explorer."

---

*Heimdal* follows Tima and Papa through the journey north — the coach to London Victoria, the meeting with Monika in St James's Park, the Arlanda train, Skansen, the sleeper train from Stockholm to Östersund, Lillsjön, and finally Nain waiting on the platform.
