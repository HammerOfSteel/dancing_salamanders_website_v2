*An Ebbw Song* is a novella following the river Ebbw through story and memory.

---

## Excerpt

The river Ebbw does not hurry. It moves with the patience of something that has been moving through this valley since before the towns arrived — before the iron, before the coal, before the terraces on the hillside and the slag heaps that are now green again. It remembers a longer time.

We walked the path from Risca toward Crosskeys, Tima and Papa and I, on a morning in late April. Tima was four. She walked with the intensity she brought to everything — small feet finding each stone deliberately, her running commentary constant and specific: "that's a big stone," "there's a bird, look, Papa, look —"

She stopped to look at the water.

"What does it say?" she asked.

"What do you mean?"

"The river. What is it saying?"

---

I told her about Bran the Blessed — the giant king whose story runs through the *Mabinogion*, who waded across the Irish Sea to rescue his sister Branwen. His head, cut off after the battle, continued to speak wisdom for eighty years. The seven survivors carried it from Ireland to Wales, stopping at Harlech, at Gwales, and finally burying it on the White Hill in London.

Tima listened to all of it with the patience of a child who takes stories seriously.

"Is his head in the river?" she asked.

"No. But the river carries stories."

She looked at it for a long time. Then: "I want to give it my name."

She crouched at the bank and said, in a clear voice: "My name is Tima."

The river moved. The water kept going.

"Did it hear?" she asked.

"Yes," I said.

---

*An Ebbw Song* is about what we carry to rivers, and what rivers carry for us.

*Author: Eric Iron*
