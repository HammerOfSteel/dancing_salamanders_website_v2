*True Colours* is a novel set in and around Newport and Risca, South Wales.

---

## Opening

The bus from Newport always smelled of damp coats and the particular tired quality of Friday afternoon. Peter had learned to take the window seat on the left, where the hill came into view just after the roundabout and you could see, on a clear day, the dark outline of Twmbarlwm against the sky.

He had been in Wales for three months. Long enough to know the café on Risca high street where the coffee was better than it had any right to be. Long enough to know the walk along the canal on Sunday mornings, when the light came in low and pale and the swans moved with the deliberate slowness of things that had nowhere to be.

He had come for a short contract and stayed. This is the story of why.

---

The canal path in early October is a particular kind of beautiful — the colours on the turning, the air just cold enough to feel like a beginning. Papa walked ahead with the kind of confidence that comes from knowing every yard of a route. Peter followed, and between them the conversation had the ease of people who have found, slowly, that they understand each other.

"You'll stay," Papa said. It was not a question.

"I don't know."

"You will."

Later, in the cottage at the edge of the village, with the fire lit and the windows dark against the early evening, Peter thought about what it meant to stay somewhere. To choose a place. Not because you were born there, not because family obligation held you, but because the place had become — somehow, without your quite deciding it — yours.

---

*True Colours* follows Peter and Papa through the seasons of a South Welsh year, and the arrival of Tima, who changes everything.
