*An Old Refrain* is a novella set in Ty Sign and Tredegar Park, Newport.

---

## Excerpt

Tima was twenty-two months old the December we took her to the Christmas market at the Tredegar Grounds.

She had not been to a market before. She walked between us holding both our hands — her grip the particular fierce grip of a small person navigating an uncertain world — and looked at everything with the focused attention she brought to everything new: the lights, the stalls, the man selling roasted chestnuts, the sound of a brass band somewhere in the middle distance.

"What's that?" she said, pointing at the lights on the large tree.

"Stars," said Steph.

Tima considered this. "Why?"

"Because we put them there to remind ourselves they're up above."

Another consideration. Then: "Okay."

---

We stayed longer than we meant to. The cold came in from the Transporter Bridge side and Tima was red-cheeked and bright-eyed and entirely uninterested in leaving. She had found the carousel.

She rode it three times, waving each time she came around to where we stood. The third time she did not wave — she was too busy watching the other children.

Walking back to the car through Ty Sign, the streets quiet and the windows warm, she fell asleep in the carrier before we reached the top road. Her weight, solid and warm and perfectly trusting, pressed against me in the cold December dark.

I did not want to arrive home.
