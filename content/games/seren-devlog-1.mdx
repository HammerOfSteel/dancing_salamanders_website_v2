Seren started life as a 3D RPG. That version had dungeons, combat mechanics, and all the scaffolding that comes with that genre. The more I worked on it, the more I felt I was building the wrong thing. The story I wanted to tell — something quiet and strange, rooted in Welsh folklore and the texture of grief — didn't need mechanics that encouraged action and combat. It needed space. It needed stillness.

So I changed the format.

## Why Visual Novel

A visual novel is not a lesser form because of what it lacks. It removes the noise that would distract from what matters: the story, the characters, the world. Every frame is intentional. Every piece of music is chosen. The player cannot bulldoze their way through with stats — they have to be present, to read, to feel.

Seren is about a girl named Seren (the Welsh word for *star*) who is navigating loss and transition in a world that sits at the boundary between the known and the mythic. That story wants contemplation, not combat.

## Welsh Folklore

The world of Seren draws on the *Mabinogion* — the collection of Welsh tales that is one of the oldest bodies of prose literature in Britain. The *Mabinogion* is full of threshold moments: people who pass between worlds, animals who are people, landscapes that are alive with meaning.

From the *Mabinogion*:

- **Rhiannon** — a figure of queenship, sorrow, and endurance, wrongly accused, whose story is about bearing injustice with dignity until the truth can be known
- **Branwen** — a story of displacement, of a woman used as a political object, of the long cost of violence on families and nations
- **Pryderi** — who is present in all four branches, a thread of continuity through transformation and loss

These stories are not costuming. They are structural. Seren's journey follows the shape of an initiatory descent — going into the dark, meeting what lives there, returning changed.

## Alchemical Symbolism

The other major structural layer is alchemy — not the literal pursuit of gold, but alchemy understood as a symbolic map of transformation. The stages: *nigredo* (blackening, dissolution), *albedo* (whitening, purification), *citrinitas* (yellowing, the dawn), *rubedo* (reddening, completion). These describe a process of identity death and rebirth.

Seren moves through these stages. The game's colour palette and visual language shift with them. This is not explained to the player — it is there to be felt rather than decoded.

## Art Pipeline with Meshy AI

One of the practical challenges of this project has been art assets. I am not a 2D illustrator in the conventional sense. I have been using [Meshy AI](https://www.meshy.ai/) to generate and refine character and environment art — starting from reference descriptions and iterating toward the specific visual language the game needs.

The combination of AI-assisted generation with intentional art direction is proving to be a workable pipeline. The key is specificity: vague prompts produce generic results. Detailed, emotionally-specific descriptions with clear stylistic references produce material that can be refined into something distinctive.

## What's Next

The next phase is building the core narrative structure in Godot and completing the visual language of the first act — establishing the texture of Seren's world before she crosses the threshold into what comes after.
